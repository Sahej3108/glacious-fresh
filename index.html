General

Cold chain logistics is the process of transporting and storing temperature-sensitive goods - such as fresh food, frozen products, dairy, seafood and pharmaceuticals - in a continuously temperature-controlled environment. The "chain" refers to the unbroken sequence of refrigerated storage and transport from origin to final destination. Any break in the chain risks spoilage, product loss or - in the case of medicines - a serious safety hazard.

We handle a broad range of temperature-sensitive cargo including fresh fruit and vegetables, frozen meat and seafood, dairy products, confectionery, ready meals, vaccines and biologics, clinical trial materials, pharmaceutical drugs, floral products, and specialty chemicals. If your cargo has a temperature requirement, we can handle it.

Our fleet and facilities cover the full cold chain spectrum: Deep Frozen (-25°C to -18°C), Frozen (-18°C to -12°C), Chilled (0°C to 8°C), Controlled Ambient (8°C to 15°C), and Ambient with monitoring (up to +25°C). Many of our vehicles feature dual-zone capability, allowing different temperature profiles within a single vehicle.

Yes. Glacious Fresh is the dedicated cold chain and temperature-controlled logistics brand operating under the Gale Worldwide Movers group. This gives us the operational depth and global network of a large logistics company, combined with specialist cold chain expertise, technology and compliance capabilities.

Technology and Monitoring

Every vehicle in our refrigerated fleet is equipped with IoT-enabled temperature sensors that log readings every 5 minutes and transmit data in real time to our cloud monitoring platform. You can access live temperature data for your shipment through our online tracking portal. Any temperature excursion outside the agreed range triggers an immediate automated alert to both our operations team and the customer.

You can enter this on our Track Shipment page to view real-time location, current temperature, transit milestones and estimated delivery time. Temperature compliance reports can also be downloaded directly from the tracking portal after delivery.

Yes. For pharmaceutical and life sciences shipments, we provide a full Temperature Excursion Report and Compliance Certificate for every consignment. This document includes a continuous temperature log, any excursion events (and corrective actions taken), vehicle pre-cooling records and digital proof of delivery. These reports are accepted by GDP auditors and regulatory authorities.

Pricing, Compliance and Safety

You can request a quote through our Contact page or by calling our team directly. To get the most accurate quote, please provide: the type of cargo, required temperature range, origin and destination, approximate weight and volume, and preferred service type (road, air or sea). We aim to respond to all quote requests within 2 business hours.

We hold HACCP certification for food safety management, GDP (Good Distribution Practice) certification for pharmaceutical cold chain, ISO 22000 for food safety systems, ISO 9001 for quality management, and IATA accreditation as a regulated air cargo agent. Our drivers and warehouse staff are also trained and certified in cold chain handling and food safety procedures. Copies of our current certificates are available on request.

Our monitoring systems trigger an immediate alert if any excursion occurs. Our operations team will contact you within 15 minutes to assess the situation and agree on a course of action, which may include diverting to the nearest cold store, arranging emergency replacement cooling, or - if necessary - holding the cargo for quality assessment. All incidents are fully documented. We also carry cargo liability insurance, and our team will assist with any claims process.

Yes. We offer cargo insurance as part of our service or can arrange it on your behalf through our insurance partners. Coverage can include all-risk transit insurance, temperature excursion damage, and specific pharmaceutical product liability cover. Please discuss your requirements with our team when requesting a quote so we can include the appropriate insurance in your proposal.
